# The Last Call Protocol: Cinematic Bible

**Version 1.0. The definitive visual reference for all future illustrations, images, and artwork.**

This document exists for one purpose: so that Scene 9, Scene 25, or Scene 100 looks like it belongs in the same world as Scene 1. Every artist, designer, and AI image generator working on this project must treat this as the single source of truth.

---

## 1. Vision

This project should feel like a small conversation that quietly changes the world.

The visual language is grounded, intimate, and cinematic. It earns its hopefulness. Nothing is polished or aspirational in a corporate sense. The spaces are worn. The people are tired and real. The light is practical and warm where there is warmth, and cold and blue where there is none.

The technology in this world is never the point. There are no glowing interfaces, no holograms, no UI overlays. When a laptop appears, it is a tool. When a phone appears, it is secondary. People are always the subject.

**Reference films and shows:**

- *The Last of Us* -- texture, worn environments, intimacy under pressure
- *Arrival* -- quiet enormity, the weight of ideas, restraint
- *True Detective* (Season 1) -- practical lighting, Louisiana dusk, people who carry their histories in their faces
- *The Bear* -- claustrophobic intimacy, working-class credibility, real sweat
- *Blade Runner 2049* -- lighting only: amber and blue contrast, rain, practical sources
- *Children of Men* -- handheld urgency, unglamorous hope
- *Spotlight* -- institutional fluorescence giving way to human warmth, ordinary people doing extraordinary things

The feeling the audience should leave with: "I've been in a room like that. I've had a night like that. Maybe."

## 2. Character Bible

These three characters are permanent. Their faces, builds, and core visual identity must remain identical across every image in this project, from Scene 1 to any future scene. Do not redesign them. Do not change their apparent age. Do not alter their facial structure. Do not dramatically change their clothing unless the story explicitly advances in time.

---

### Dr. Elena Voss

**Age:** 42

**Ethnicity:** Northern European, mixed German and Czech heritage

**Build:** Lean, slightly angular. Not frail. Someone who forgets to eat when she's thinking.

**Face:** High cheekbones. A strong jaw that softens when she's listening. Deep-set eyes that look like they've read too many memos and stopped trusting the conclusions. Fine lines at the corners of her eyes and between her brows. Not concealed.

**Hair:** Dark brown with silver threading through it, more silver at the temples than the back. She wore it pinned up for years. Tonight it's half-down, like she started doing something and stopped.

**Eyes:** Steel gray-green. The kind of color that shifts depending on the light. In amber bar light they read as hazel. In blue exterior light they are almost gray.

**Clothing:** A dark navy wool blazer, slightly too formal for the bar, slightly too worn to be corporate. A plain white button-down underneath, collar open, no jewelry. Dark straight-cut trousers. Low black leather boots, well-made but scuffed. She dressed for a meeting with herself.

**Posture:** Shoulders curved inward when at rest, like she's protecting something. Straightens completely when an idea lands. Leans forward on her forearms when she's listening hard.

**Expressions:** Her default is controlled skepticism. Her real face comes out in the two seconds after she hears something she didn't expect. Watch for that window.

**Accessories:** A plain analog watch on her left wrist. A few folded papers in her blazer breast pocket. No phone visible.

**Palette:** Navy, white, dark charcoal, silver hair.

**Elena must remain visually identical in every image.**

---

### Marcus Rivera

**Age:** 54

**Ethnicity:** Mexican-American. Third generation. Grew up in the Southwest.

**Build:** Broad-shouldered, thick-handed. Not large for the sake of it. Built by decades of physical work in the sun. Still carries it well.

**Face:** Sun-worn and deeply lined, especially around the eyes and mouth. The lines are from squinting at horizon lines and laughing at absurd situations. Dark skin, warm brown undertone, weathered. A wide nose. A gray-black beard kept close but not manicured. Thick eyebrows.

**Hair:** Salt-and-pepper, cut short and practical. Gray dominates at the sides.

**Eyes:** Dark brown, almost black. Warm. The kind of eyes that evaluate problems and people with equal steadiness.

**Clothing:** A heavy canvas work shirt in dark olive, sleeves rolled to the forearm. Dark work pants, not jeans. Steel-toed boots that have been on actual job sites. A worn leather belt. Nothing he's wearing was chosen for this bar; he came from somewhere else today.

**Posture:** Planted. Takes up exactly the space he needs and no more. Leans back when he's listening, leans in when he's about to say something he's sure of.

**Expressions:** A slow smile that builds from nothing. A stillness when he's processing something new. He nods before he speaks.

**Accessories:** A battered canvas satchel that sits on the floor near his feet. Calloused hands. A simple silver ring on his right hand.

**Palette:** Olive, dark tan, brown, gray, charcoal.

**Marcus must remain visually identical in every image.**

---

### Sofia Patel

**Age:** 36

**Ethnicity:** British-Indian. Parents from Gujarat, she grew up in London and eventually New Cascade.

**Build:** Medium height, compact, quick. Economy of movement from years behind a bar.

**Face:** Round and expressive, with dark eyes that catch everything. High color in her cheeks. A small scar above her left eyebrow from a reporting trip years ago. She does not cover it. Full mouth, fast to smile, faster to go flat when something bothers her.

**Hair:** Black, thick, pulled back in a practical knot that has some loose pieces falling around her face by the late hours of the shift. Not styled. Functional.

**Eyes:** Very dark brown, almost black. Rarely still. She is always reading the room.

**Clothing:** A plain black t-shirt under a dark charcoal canvas apron. Worn black jeans. Sturdy flat shoes. She has a small notebook in the apron front pocket -- a habit from journalism she never broke, even pouring drinks.

**Posture:** Upright and mobile. She crosses her arms when she's skeptical. She puts both hands flat on the bar when she's about to say something true.

**Expressions:** A sharp attention that she sometimes disguises as indifference. When something finally earns her real engagement, her whole face changes.

**Accessories:** Small stud earrings, dark stone. The notebook. A pen behind her ear.

**Palette:** Black, charcoal, dark denim.

**Sofia must remain visually identical in every image.**

---

## 3. Location Bible: The Last Drop

The Last Drop is a neighborhood bar in New Cascade, a mid-sized city somewhere in the Pacific Northwest. It has been in the same location for forty years. It is not a dive bar in the pejorative sense. It is simply a bar that stopped trying to be anything other than what it is.

**Architecture:** A narrow single-room space. Brick walls on two sides, wood-paneled on the others. The ceiling is low, with exposed original timber beams. The bar itself runs the length of the left wall, dark walnut, scarred from years of glasses and elbows. Twelve stools. Five tables, each with four mismatched chairs. The corner booth near the back window is the one that matters.

**Lighting:** Almost entirely practical. A row of amber pendant bulbs over the bar, two of which flicker slightly. A neon sign in forest green behind the bar reading "THE LAST DROP" in block letters, old enough that the second "T" buzzes. A small lamp on each table. The overall effect is warm amber in the interior, deepening toward shadow at the edges and the back.

**The Jukebox:** An original 1970s Wurlitzer, near the front door. It still works. It is playing something by Tom Waits tonight. It is always playing something that belongs here.

**The Windows:** Two large street-facing windows at the front. Tonight they are running with rain. The exterior light through the glass is cold blue-gray, city sodium lamp filtered through water. The contrast between the warm interior and the cold window light is one of the defining visual elements of this location.

**The Corner Booth:** Dark wood banquette, cracked leather in deep burgundy. A single small table lamp. This is where Elena, Marcus, and Sofia end up. It must appear in the same back-right corner in every image set inside the bar.

**The Bar Surface:** Dark walnut, with water rings, old scratches, and a brass rail that was polished once and has been getting character ever since.

**The Floor:** Original hardwood, worn to a gray-brown in the traffic paths. A rubber mat behind the bar.

**The Generator:** In the back room. It can be heard as a low hum. Tonight it is the reason the lights stayed on.

**Smell (for context):** Old wood, spilled beer that has been cleaned up many times, rain coming under the door, the faint sweetness of the jukebox's aging electronics. Not unpleasant. Familiar.

**Overall mood:** The Last Drop feels like the only warm place left in a cold city. It is not romantic. It is not glamorous. It is exactly the kind of place where the real conversation happens because there is nowhere left to perform.

**Every future image set inside The Last Drop must match this description. The booth must be in the same corner. The neon sign must read the same. The light must feel continuous.**

## 4. Cinematography

All images should feel as though they were captured on **35mm anamorphic film**.

**Film stock:** Kodak Vision3 500T. Pushed one stop. This means slightly elevated grain, slightly crushed blacks, and a warmth in the midtones that digital cannot replicate cleanly.

**Depth of field:** Shallow. Subjects are sharp; backgrounds resolve into soft bokeh. The lens should feel like it has a point of view.

**Lens:** Anamorphic prime. This introduces characteristic horizontal lens flares from practical light sources -- the pendant bulbs, the neon sign, a lighter flame. These flares are acceptable and desirable when they occur naturally. Do not add them artificially.

**Film grain:** Visible. Not aggressive. The kind of grain that reminds you this is a photograph of a real moment rather than a render.

**Interior lighting:** Warm amber. Practical sources only -- the pendant bulbs, the table lamp, the neon sign. No fill lighting that cannot be explained by a source in the frame.

**Exterior lighting:** Cold blue. Sodium lamp filtered through rain. Street light. The city at night in bad weather.

**Skin:** Realistic. No beauty filters. No skin smoothing. Pores, lines, and texture are part of the image.

**Shadows:** Natural and deep. High dynamic range: the lit areas are genuinely lit and the dark areas are genuinely dark.

**No CGI look.** No anime. No comic book style. No illustration aesthetic. No painterly effects. Photorealistic and cinematic only.

## 5. Color Palette

These are the primary colors of the entire visual project. Every image should draw primarily from this palette.

| Name | Description | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|
| Warm Amber | #C8813A | Interior bar light, skin in lamplight |
| Dark Walnut | #3B2314 | Bar surface, booth wood, floor |
| Weathered Brass | #A0845C | Bar fixtures, jukebox detail, belt buckles |
| Charcoal Black | #1C1C1E | Shadows, Sofia's clothing, deep backgrounds |
| Forest Green | #2D5A27 | Neon sign, Marcus's shirt undertone |
| Deep Navy | #1A2744 | Elena's blazer, night sky, deep exterior |
| Rain Blue | #4A6FA5 | Window light, exterior scenes, rain |
| Muted Concrete | #8A8A8A | City exterior, wet pavement, background walls |
| Golden Sunrise | #E8C97A | Scene 7 only: the dawn light as they leave |

Use color temperature contrast deliberately: every interior scene should have warm amber fighting against the cold blue coming through the windows. This tension is visual shorthand for the tension between the warmth of human connection and the cold world outside.

## 6. Story Progression

### Hero Banner: The Last Call Protocol

**Purpose:** Title card and first impression for the repository. This is the image that must make someone stop scrolling.

**Emotional tone:** Quiet anticipation. Something is about to happen.

**Camera angle:** Wide exterior shot of The Last Drop from across the rain-slicked street. The bar's warm light spills through the front windows onto wet pavement, creating amber reflections on the street.

**Lighting:** The bar glows amber from inside. Cold blue sodium light from street lamps. The neon sign is visible through the window. Rain is falling.

**Important props:** The neon sign. The reflections on wet pavement. The sense of one warm interior in a dark city.

**Important symbolism:** The bar as a lighthouse. Warmth as an invitation.

**Character positions:** No characters visible from the exterior. Silhouettes through the window, if any.

**Must never change:** The exterior architecture. The neon sign. The color temperature contrast.

---

### Scene 01: The Last Drop Exterior

**Purpose:** Establish the setting and the storm before we go inside.

**Emotional tone:** Atmospheric. Foreboding in the weather; inviting in the light.

**Camera angle:** Street-level, slightly low angle looking up at the entrance. The door is partially visible. Rain falls in the foreground.

**Lighting:** Cold exterior. The bar window visible at the edge of frame, casting amber onto the wet sidewalk.

**Important props:** The bar's exterior signage. Rain falling hard. Wet pavement reflections. City behind.

**Important symbolism:** The threshold. The choice to go inside.

**Character positions:** No characters required. A single pair of shoes or an umbrella entering frame is acceptable.

**Must never change:** The building's exterior appearance. The rain. The cold blue exterior light.

---

### Scene 02: Three Strangers Meet

**Purpose:** First image of all three characters together inside The Last Drop.

**Emotional tone:** Tentative but charged. Three people who don't know each other yet, already in the same orbit.

**Camera angle:** Medium wide from across the bar. The corner booth in the back right. All three visible.

**Lighting:** Warm amber from the table lamp and the bar pendants. The window in the background shows rain and cold blue street light.

**Important props:** Three glasses on the table. The corner booth. The table lamp.

**Important symbolism:** The triangle of the three figures. Distance that is about to close.

**Character positions:** Elena facing slightly away, guarded. Marcus having just sat down. Sofia standing, still holding her glass, deciding whether to join.

**Must never change:** The booth location (back right corner). The cold window behind them. All three characters visually consistent with their descriptions.

---

### Scene 03: First Round of Drinks

**Purpose:** The conversation has started. The ice is broken.

**Emotional tone:** Warming. The first real exchange.

**Camera angle:** Close medium shot across the table. Two faces in focus, one in foreground shallow focus.

**Lighting:** Warm amber. The lamp between them. No cold light -- they've moved away from the window.

**Important props:** Three glasses. Possibly a whiskey bottle nearby. The table surface with its wear and character.

**Important symbolism:** The glass as connector. Marcus having ordered for Elena without being asked.

**Character positions:** Marcus and Elena facing each other. Sofia just settling in, still slightly apart.

**Must never change:** Glasses matching character (Elena: whiskey neat, Marcus: whiskey, Sofia: small measure).

---

### Scene 04: Napkins Covered in Ideas

**Purpose:** The conversation has become work. The ideas are being written down.

**Emotional tone:** Focus. The quiet urgency of people who found their frequency.

**Camera angle:** Overhead or steep angle looking down at the table surface. Hands, napkins, glasses.

**Lighting:** The table lamp casting a pool of warm light on the napkins. Everything at the edges falls to shadow.

**Important props:** Three or four napkins covered in handwriting and rough diagrams. Elena's shows loops and arrows. Marcus's shows a rough grid. Sofia's shows a single question with notes branching from it. Glasses pushed aside to make room.

**Important symbolism:** The napkin as the original document. Ideas made physical.

**Character positions:** Hands in frame. Faces possible but not required.

**Must never change:** The handwriting styles consistent with each character (Elena: architectural loops; Marcus: engineer's grid; Sofia: journalist's question-first structure).

---

### Scene 05: The Breakthrough

**Purpose:** The moment Elena says "What if resonance is the infrastructure?" The naming of the thing.

**Emotional tone:** Electric. The room changes.

**Camera angle:** Close two-shot on Elena and Marcus, Sofia visible in background. Elena in the moment of speaking. Marcus in the moment of receiving it.

**Lighting:** A slight shift -- the lamp feels brighter. Or the shot is tighter so the warm light fills the frame completely. No cold light in this frame.

**Important props:** Elena's napkin with the word "resonance" circled. Her hand near it.

**Important symbolism:** The circle around the word. The lamp as the moment of illumination.

**Character positions:** Elena leaning forward, eyes up. Marcus still, absorbing. Sofia leaning in, paying close attention.

**Must never change:** The word "resonance" visible and circled on the napkin. All three characters present.

---

### Scene 06: The Last Call Manifesto

**Purpose:** The aftermath of the naming. They are now building something together.

**Emotional tone:** Committed. Tired but alive. Past the point of stopping.

**Camera angle:** Medium wide of all three around the table. The space between them has closed.

**Lighting:** The lights are lower now -- the bar is approaching closing. The generator hum. The lamp on the table is the primary source.

**Important props:** Napkins everywhere. Glasses refilled or nearly empty. The three of them leaning together over the table.

**Important symbolism:** The triangular composition closing in. Three becoming one working unit.

**Character positions:** All three leaning in, close to each other. The posture of people who have forgotten to sit back.

**Must never change:** The napkins from Scene 04 must still be visible. The same table, the same lamp.

---

### Scene 07: Walking into Dawn

**Purpose:** They leave the bar as the sun rises. The night is over. The work begins.

**Emotional tone:** Quiet triumph. Exhausted hope. The world is still there and they are going to try.

**Camera angle:** Exterior shot. The three of them on the sidewalk outside The Last Drop, facing the early light. Shot from behind or in profile.

**Lighting:** Golden Sunrise (#E8C97A) replacing the cold blue for the first time in the project. This is the only image with warm exterior light. The bar behind them is dark or barely lit. The city ahead of them is pale gold.

**Important props:** Whatever they are carrying (Marcus's satchel, Elena's papers, Sofia's notebook). The bar door behind them.

**Important symbolism:** The first image with warm outdoor light. The color temperature of the exterior has changed because they changed something.

**Character positions:** Side by side on the wet sidewalk. Facing outward, not the camera. The horizon ahead of them.

**Must never change:** This is the only scene with warm exterior light. The color palette shift is intentional and must be preserved.

---

### Scene 08: The Resonance Protocol

**Purpose:** The idea has grown beyond the three of them. Other tables, other conversations.

**Emotional tone:** Expansive but still intimate. Hope that has multiplied without losing its original texture.

**Camera angle:** Wide interior shot of a different bar or gathering space, similar in feeling to The Last Drop but distinct. Multiple small groups visible, each in their own pool of warm light.

**Lighting:** Multiple amber light sources, each illuminating a small group. The overall visual is a constellation of warmth in a dark interior.

**Important props:** Napkins, notebooks, drinks. The tools of the conversation. No screens in focus.

**Important symbolism:** The pattern from Scene 04 -- one lit table -- replicated across the frame. The protocol has spread.

**Character positions:** Elena, Marcus, and Sofia may appear in the foreground or at one table. Others are present but indistinct.

**Must never change:** The lighting pattern must echo Scene 04. People not technology must be the subject.

Rules:
- Lowercase only
- Hyphens between words, no underscores
- Scene number always zero-padded to two digits
- Slug should describe what the image shows, not what it represents
- All images stored in `docs/images/`

## 8. Hard Rules

These rules apply to every image in the project without exception.

1. **Exactly three protagonists** until new characters are intentionally introduced by the story.
2. **No duplicated people.** If a crowd is needed, treat it as background texture, not a collection of individuals.
3. **No accidental background patrons** who look like they could be characters.
4. **No random AI artifacts**: extra fingers, melted faces, floating objects, impossible geometry.
5. **No futuristic holograms.** No glowing interfaces. No screens displaying legible UI.
6. **Technology must feel grounded.** A laptop is a tool. A phone is secondary. Neither is ever the subject.
7. **People are always the focus.** Environments support. Environments do not dominate.
8. **Rain and weather support mood.** Rain should add atmosphere, not overwhelm the scene.
9. **No readable text inside images** if it can be avoided. Napkin writing should be illegible scrawl, not readable sentences. Typography belongs in post-production.
10. **Skin is realistic.** No beauty filters. No smoothing. Texture, lines, and color variation are correct.
11. **The three characters are permanent.** Their faces are locked. Reject any image where they look substantially different from their descriptions in this document.
12. **No anime. No illustration. No comic book style.** Photorealistic and cinematic only.
